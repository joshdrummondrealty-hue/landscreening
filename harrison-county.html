02

What "already zoned" actually means here

Harrison County runs a Unified Development Code plus a SmartCode (transect) overlay along key corridors. Districts sort into three buckets for a frontage-commercial play. Confirm the current code — the county amends it periodically.

Ready

Buy & build — by right

  • C-2General Commercial — highway retail, pads, services
  • C-1Neighborhood / limited commercial
  • B-2Business — arterial commercial nodes
  • T5 / T6SmartCode urban-center & core transects
Frontage retail/commercial is a permitted use — no rezone needed. Best fit for your screen.
Conditional

Zoned, but needs a step

  • C-3Highway/heavy commercial — some uses conditional
  • T4General urban transect — use-specific approvals
  • M-1Light industrial — if the play is flex/warehouse
  • PUDPlanned unit dev — site-plan review
Zoned for commercial-ish use but expect a conditional-use permit or site-plan hearing. Price the delay in.
Not ready

Would need a rezone

  • A-1General Agriculture (15ac+ intent)
  • R-1Single-family residential
  • R-2Low-density residential
  • E-1Estate / rural residential
Cheap frontage often sits here. Viable only if you'll carry a rezone — longer, political, no guarantee.

05

Pull the full set yourself — county GIS query recipes

Harrison County publishes an authoritative zoning map service. These are copy-paste queries to pull every commercially-zoned parcel, then intersect with roads for frontage. Run them from a browser or QGIS; the endpoints require no login.

1 · All ready-zoned parcels (C-1/C-2/B-2)

ArcGIS REST query against the county zoning layer. Adjust the field name (ZONE/ZONING) to match the layer schema.

# Harrison County zoning MapServer — Zoning Districts layer
https://geo.co.harrison.ms.us/server/rest/services/District/Zoning/MapServer/1/query
  ?where=ZONE IN ('C-1','C-2','B-2')
  &outFields=*
  &returnGeometry=true
  &f=geojson

2 · Inspect the schema first

Confirm the real zoning field name + coded values before querying. Open this and read fields[] and any domain.

https://geo.co.harrison.ms.us/server/rest/services/District/Zoning/MapServer/1?f=json

# Alt (AGO-hosted feature service):
https://geo.co.harrison.ms.us/server/rest/services/AGO/HarrisonCounty_Zoning/FeatureServer/0?f=json

3 · Frontage filter — parcels touching a highway

Spatial intersect in QGIS: load zoning + the MS DOT / county road centerlines, buffer roads ~15 m, select zoned parcels whose boundary intersects the buffer. That set = zoned and road-frontage.

# QGIS Processing recipe
1. Add layer  → zoning MapServer/1  (filter: ZONE IN ('C-1','C-2','B-2'))
2. Add layer  → road centerlines (county GIS / MS DOT)
3. Buffer     → roads, 15 m, dissolve
4. Extract by location → zoned parcels that INTERSECT road buffer
5. Field calc → frontage_ft = length of shared boundary * 3.281
6. Filter     → frontage_ft > 150  (drop flag-lots / slivers)

4 · Saved aggregator searches (owner + asking price)

The GIS gives you zoning + frontage; these give you what's for sale + who to call. Bookmark and check weekly.

07

Due-diligence kit — Gulf Coast edition

Before earnest money goes hard. On the coast, flood zone and wetlands make or break the yield you just calculated — check them first.

AFlood & elevation

  • Pull the FEMA flood zone — AE / VE coastal zones drive fill, BFE & insurance deal-killer
  • Base Flood Elevation vs. site grade → fill cost per lot
  • Check post-Katrina advisory / V-zone velocity limits

BWetlands & environment

  • USACE / NWI wetlands overlay — feeds your "unbuildable %" yield hit
  • Order a jurisdictional determination if any doubt
  • Phase I ESA if prior commercial/ag use

CAccess & frontage

  • Curb-cut / access permit — MS DOT for US-49 & I-10, county for local roads
  • Confirm legal frontage vs. easement-only access
  • Turn-lane / deceleration requirements at high VPD

DZoning & entitlements

  • Written zoning verification letter from the county
  • Setbacks, buffers, height & parking ratios for the district
  • Any SmartCode transect overlay conditions

EUtilities

  • Sewer vs. septic — Harrison County Utility Authority availability
  • Water & fire-flow at the frontage
  • Power / gas capacity; distance to tie-in

FTitle & survey

  • Title commitment — easements, ROW, mineral rights
  • Boundary + topo survey; confirm acreage vs. tax roll
  • Existing liens / back taxes on the parcel
08

From lead to contract

The repeatable workflow once a parcel clears the screen above.

Identify the owner of record

Pull the parcel from the county tax-assessor online map → owner name + mailing address + last sale. Cross-check acreage against the listing.

Skip-trace & qualify

Phone/email for the owner (or the LLC's registered agent via MS Secretary of State). Note whether it's listed with an agent or a for-sale-by-owner you can approach directly.

Send the opener

Short, specific, non-lowball. Reference the parcel by frontage and zoning so they know you've done homework.

"I'm a local land buyer focused on the US-49 / I-10 corridor. I'm interested in your ~13.5-acre frontage parcel on Hwy 49 South. If you'd consider a clean cash offer with a short due-diligence window, I'd like to send a letter of intent this week — what number would get your attention?"

LOI → contract with contingencies

Non-binding LOI first, then a purchase agreement with feasibility contingencies covering every item in the DD kit — zoning verification, flood/wetlands, access permit, and utilities — before earnest money goes hard.
