The Buy Box

Three ways to get paid, in order of speed. Everything gets wholesaled or flipped to the builder — no infrastructure, no development risk.

Program 1 · Fastest

Spot Lots (Scattered Lots)

  • Vacant single lots in existing subdivisions — utilities and roads already in.
  • Builder pays $75K–$120K per lot (Pines of Perdido range). Orange Beach / Gulf Shores commands more.
  • Contract the lot, assign the contract for a $2K–$7K fee. Simultaneous close, ~60 days.
  • Builder wants a 25% margin — price off the last comp in the subdivision, not the highest ask ever.
  • Their goal: 200/yr. They closed 45 in year one. The demand is there.
Program 2 · The Niche

Road-Frontage Minis

  • 5–25 lots cut along an existing county road. No new roads — survey, subdivide, close.
  • Rule of thumb: ~120 ft of frontage per lot on county roads (driveway spacing). Bigger lots = no competition with 60-ft subdivision inventory. 3-acre lots are fine.
  • Want water + electric at the street. Septic and well are OK — "we'll get in the boonies with it."
  • Big rural parcel? Ask to buy just the frontage: "Can I buy 2,000 feet of your frontage?" Owner keeps the back land (and any commercial corner).
  • Killing the development cost — not the land cost — is what makes these pencil.
Program 3 · Own Account

Small Developments

  • 5–40 acres, up to ~25 lots — one-man-show size, run through the C-corp.
  • Small deals approve easier: 22 lots doesn't pull a crowd at Planning Commission; 300 lots pulls two towns.
  • Ten small deals beat one big one — spread footprint, spread absorption risk.
  • Bigger opportunity than 25 lots? Partner it or flip it entitled.
  • Entitlement package target: Phase 1 ESA + boundary/topo survey + plat. Nothing heavier.

Kill Screens — check these before you dial

Two minutes of screening saves a wasted week. The Deal Score in the pipeline runs off these.

  • Bay Minette school zone = dead. Anywhere else in the county works — "I-10 all the way down, and up in Loxley." Check the Baldwin County school zone locator before anything else.
  • Trailer-heavy area = dead — except beach markets (Orange Beach / Gulf Shores), where old trailers are teardowns waiting on a million-dollar house.
  • Wetlands / swamp: if it looks wet on the wetlands mapper, it is. No geotech shortcuts — that's someone else's lawsuit.
  • Survey encroachments: look for buildings, car storage, fences over the line. Adverse-possession claims kill closings.
  • Price benchmark: central-county acreage (Silverhill area) trades $18K–$25K/acre. Out-of-town money is paying $50K and not closing — let them burn the year, stay in touch, be there when the listing dies.
  • Utilities: water + electric at the street is the target. Public sewer is gravy. No sewer? Confirm septic feasibility (soil test) before contract — see County Rules below.
  • Frontage math: frontage ÷ 120 ft ≈ lot count on county road. 800+ ft of frontage with water = automatic call.
  • Relationship rule: any lead the builder hands over goes back to the builder. Handshake is the whole business.

Baldwin County Rules Cheat Sheet

The regulatory facts that make or break a road-front subdivide. Verified July 2026 — always confirm current rules before contract.

Subdivision regulations (current version adopted Jan 7, 2025)

  • Exempt divisions (no subdivision process): family division deeded to immediate family (parent, sibling, spouse, child); a one-time split into two parcels if the parcel has existed un-divided since Feb 1, 1984; splits where both resulting parcels are ≥35 acres; common-lot-line moves that create no new parcel.
  • Driveway spacing on county roads: 120-ft intervals or greater — this is why road-front lots pencil at ~120 ft wide.
  • Everything else runs through Planning & Zoning as a minor or major subdivision — plat, review, Planning Commission where required. Small lot counts move faster and quieter.
  • Zoning varies by planning district; some districts are unzoned. Verify district + zoning (e.g., R-2 already in place = submit a plat and go).
  • Confirm current lot-width and minor-plat thresholds per district: Baldwin County Planning & Zoning, (251) 580-1655.

Septic / onsite sewage (ADPH Chapter 420-3-1)

  • Soil test first: a licensed soil professional's test goes to the county health department with the permit application. No perc, no deal — make offers contingent on it.
  • Setbacks: disposal field ≥ 100 ft from any private well, ≥ 5 ft from property lines — plus a reserved replacement field area on the lot.
  • Sewer mandate: lots under 1 acre with public sewer available must connect — septic isn't an option there.
  • Minimum lot size for septic is site-specific (soil, water supply, bedrooms). Rule of thumb: bigger road-front lots (1–3 ac) clear easily; sub-half-acre lots need public water and good soil.
  • Confirm per-site: Baldwin County Health Dept environmental office, (251) 947-3618 / (251) 937-6935.

Data Sources & Search Recipes

The GIS screen the builder asked for: “pull me all parcels with 800 feet of road frontage with water and sewer.”

Primary sources

  • Baldwin County parcel viewer (KCS GIS) — ownership, PINs, mailing addresses, acreage. The workhorse.
  • Planning & Zoning — subdivision regulations and department page (agendas, rezonings, permit forms).
  • Baldwin County Board of Education — search “school zone locator.” Screen every parcel; Bay Minette zone is out.
  • ADPH Baldwin — onsite sewage — septic permitting process and contacts.
  • USFWS Wetlands Mapper — free first-pass wetlands screen.
  • FEMA flood map center — flood zone before you fall in love.

Field + owner tools

  • LandGlide — in-truck parcel lookup while driving frontage.
  • Metro Market Trends — comps for spot-lot pricing (what the last lot actually sold for).
  • Skip trace: GIS mailing address first, then TruePeopleSearch / FastPeopleSearch, then probate records for heirs. Can't find them? Knock on the door.
  • Permit / agenda tracking: your existing tracker + PermitStack feeds; watch Planning Commission agendas for rezonings that signal sellers and absorption.

Search recipes

  • Frontage hunt: in GIS, trace target county roads (south of I-10 + Loxley), flag parcels with ≥800 ft frontage, cross-check water provider service maps, then screen wetlands + school zone. Everything that survives goes in the pipeline above.
  • Spot-lot hunt: pick an older subdivision (Pines of Perdido pattern), list every vacant lot from GIS, pull owner mailing addresses, and call down the list. Tie up every one that comps.

Owner Outreach

Call first. Mail second. Knock when they won't answer. Six-month follow-ups win the deal two years later.

Cold-call opener (frontage)

“Hey [name], this is Josh Drummond — I'm a local land buyer here in Baldwin County, not a big out-of-town outfit. I was out on [road] and noticed your place. I'm not asking to buy the whole property — would you ever consider selling just some of the road frontage along [road]? You'd keep the back land and everything behind it.”

If interested: “I buy with a straightforward contract, I pay for the survey, and I close with a local title company. What would make it worth your while?”

Cold-call opener (spot lot)

“I buy vacant lots in [subdivision] — I saw you've owned yours since [year]. Most folks are paying taxes on a lot they'll never build on. I can close in about 60 days, cash, no commissions. The last lot in there sold for [comp] — I can be right around that.”

Working rules

  • Handshake rule: anything sourced from a builder lead goes back to that builder. Full stop.
  • Contracts: use the builder's spot-lot assignment contract; assignment fee paid at closing, simultaneous close ~60 days.
  • Overpriced owner? Don't chase. Note the ask, set a 60–90 day follow-up, be the call they make when the $50K/acre buyer evaporates.
  • Log every touch in the pipeline notes with a date — the six-month drip is the system.
